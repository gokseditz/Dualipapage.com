When crafting a resume, it’s essential to highlight both your hard and soft skills to present yourself as a well-rounded candidate. Hard skills are specific, teachable abilities such as programming, data analysis, or graphic design. These skills are often acquired through education or training and are easily quantifiable. On the other hand, soft skills include interpersonal abilities like communication, teamwork, and problem-solving. These are more subjective and harder to measure but are crucial for creating a positive work environment. Balancing both types of skills on your resume can demonstrate your technical proficiency and your ability to work well with others, making you a more attractive candidate to potential employers.
